TOP 2 FASHION RUNWAY LOOKS

1. Plato's Atlantis

Post-apocalyptic future where humans evolve to live underwater due to rising sea levels. Inspired by Charles Darwin's theory of evolution and climate change, the show featured models transforming from land creatures into underwater beings, a concept brought to life through digitally printed clothes, prosthetic features, and iconic armadillo shoes. The collection was presented as the first fashion show to be live-streamed online, creating a historic moment in both fashion and internet culture, and featured the debut of Lady Gaga's song "Bad Romance"

2. The Widows of Culloden

It was inspired by his Scottish ancestry and is regarded as one of his most autobiographical collections. It is named for the women widowed by the Battle of Culloden (1746), often seen as a major conflict between Scotland and England. Widows makes extensive use of the McQueen family tartan and traditional gamekeeper's tweeds, as well as other elements taken from Highland dress. Historical elements reflected the fashion of the late Victorian era and the 1950s.
